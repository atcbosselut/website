I am a second year PhD student at the University of Washington working in the Natural Language Processing Group and the Machine Learning Group. I am currently advised by Professor Yejin Choi. My research focuses on using noisy image and natural language data to build multimodal models that can learn common sense knowledge about the world and use it for generation tasks. My current projects include:

  • Learning event models and temporal structures for common scenarios using captioned photo albums that depict them
  • Learning temporal knowledge and object affordances from large-scale instructional corpora with corresponding images and video
  • Contextualizing images in accompanying text for more descriptive image caption generation

Prior to joining the University of Washington, I received a B.Eng. in Honours Electrical Engineering with a minor in Finance from McGill University in Montreal. During my time there, I had worked with Professor Ioannis Psaromiligkos, with whom I completed an honours thesis on using signal processing filters to reduce realized risk in financial equity portfolios. I was a recipient of the Alma Mater Scholarship, the Motorola Foundation Scholarship, the Brodeur-Drummond Scholarship, and the Brian Cullen Memorial Award.
